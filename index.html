Bio-Physicists

A.K.A: Analytical Research Chemist, Biochemist, Biophysics researcher, Scientist

Education Needed

  • Typically, a Bio-Physicist will need to have at least a masters' degree, with doctoral training being a legitimate possibility.
  • They will need a solid grasp with concepts shown in engineering, calculus, biology,chemistry, and other sciences, as well as most physics principals.
  • Literacy is a must for this job, as those within it will need to be able to show critical thinking skills in their language, as well as complex thought processes.
  • Having some engineering/technology knowledge is also a must, as a biophysicist will be repeatedly working with technology in order to simulate, and apply their findings.
  • While certificiations such as Hazmat Certifications aren't needed for this line of work, it generally helps to be able to work well with programs such as powerpoint, adobe, and various statistics programs in order to efficiently work, and present their findings.

Generally, Bio-Physicists will need extensive education, tenacity, and outside training in order to work in this field.

Personality Traits, Skills and Abilities

  • Similar to their education, those who work in this field will need to be able to be profecient doing complex math, often on the fly.
  • Bio-Physicists need to be able to infer from details shown in data, as well as to group peices of data into their group. Seeing hidden patterns within math often helps those who work in this field.
  • Of course, critical thinking, is needed for this job, similar for any other sort of position within the scientific world.
  • People in this area will need to be able to think about the effects of their work, and how it will b e affected in the future, and they need to be integric in their personality, as to make the most sound decisions with their work.
  • Perstistence, and Effort is required for such a rigorous field, due to the long hours, and the constant mathematical calculations.
  • Dependability and Attention to Detail are a must, so that way their work is safe to be used, and no flaw or detail within work goes untouched.

Bio-Physicists require a versatile set of skills on them as they pursue a career in this field, though they mostly revolve around mathematical prowess, and critical thinking.

Technology, and Work Expectations

At work, Bio-Physicists are expected to conduct tests in order to investigate the chemical, and physical composition of organisms, as well as conduct tests involving the study of chemical combinations and reactions, for things such as metabolism, genetics, reproduction, and growth that are found in nature. These are often used in medical fields, as serums, hormones. These findings are generally shared through presentations at scientific confrences, or through the publication of scientific articles. Often times, those who are deeply involved within Biophysics will find themselves aiding undergraduate, and graduate students who also wish to pursue this field, by supervising research done under them.

Generally, people in this field will be seen using...
  1. IBM SPSS Statistics software
  2. Minitab
  3. Microsoft Power Point, when presenting findings
  4. Adobe PhotoShop
  5. Other Graphics Software/Simulation software

Salary

Generally, people in the field of biophysics will make 103,810$ on average. However, it is not uncommon for those in the field to make much more than that, into the 200,000$s, or as low as 61,000$.

Typically, Bio-Physicists will be busy conducting research, aiding students, and utilizing multiple simulation softwares in order to display their findings, all the while being comfortably paid.
